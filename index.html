Symptoms

Symptoms of monkeypox usually start with a fever, followed by:

  • a headache,
  • muscle pain,
  • back pain,
  • swollen lymph nodes,
  • chills,
  • and fatigue.

A rash may also develop, often beginning on the face and then spreading to other parts of the body, including the genitals. This rash usually goes through different stages, sometimes looking like chickenpox or syphilis, before finally forming a scab that falls off later.

Transmission

Transmission of monkeypox between people is rare and usually only occurs through close contact with an infected individual.
The virus can enter the body through broken skin, the respiratory tract, or mucous membranes.
Person-to-person spread typically occurs through contact with clothing or linens used by an infected person, direct contact with monkeypox skin lesions or scabs, or by coughing or sneezing of an individual with a monkeypox rash.

Prevention

  1. During human monkeypox outbreaks, the most significant risk factor for monkeypox virus infection is close contact with infected persons.
  2. If possible, people who have been vaccinated against smallpox should be selected to care for the patient. Implementing standard infection control precautions is important.
  3. Unprotected contact with wild animals, especially those that are sick or dead, should be avoided. This includes contact with their meat, blood and other parts. All foods containing animal meat or parts must be thoroughly cooked before eating.
  4. Practice good hand hygiene after contact with infected animals or humans. Wash your hands with soap and water or using an alcohol-based hand sanitizer.

Vaccination

Vaccination against smallpox is generally about 85% effective in preventing monkeypox, though this number may vary depending on the vaccine used.
Prior vaccination against smallpox can usually be determined by the presence of a scar on the upper arm.
While there is currently no widely available vaccine or treatment for monkeypox, the FDA has approved and expects two options (MVA-BN and tecovirimat) to be more widely available in the coming years.

FAQ

What is an incubation period of monkeypox?

Usually from 6 to 13 days but can range from 5 to 21 days.

Is monkeypox dangerous?

Monkeypox can range from mild to severe, and tends to be more severe in populations with weaker immune systems, like children, pregnant women, and those with other health conditions.
The West African clade of monkeypox appears to cause less severe disease than the Congo Basin clade, with a case fatality rate of 3.6% compared to 10.6%.

What causes monkeypox?

There are two distinct genetic clades of monkeypox virus – the Central African (Congo Basin) clade and the West African clade. Monkeypox virus is an enveloped double-stranded DNA virus that belongs to the Orthopoxvirus genus of the Poxviridae family.

Is monkeypox transmitted through sexual contact?

There is a lack of clear evidence demonstrating that monkeypox can be transmitted through sexual contact.
However, as early cases have been predominantly found in gay and bisexual men, further studies are needed to explore this possibility in greater detail.
